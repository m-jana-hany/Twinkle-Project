Altadena’s Short Story: The Lesson from the Flames

Maral Nazarian and her husband

Listen to the recorded situation report.

I am Altadena. I'm known for being a peaceful, beautiful community by the mountains. But in January 2025, disaster hit. My big neighbor, Los Angeles, was still talking about the Palisades fire when the flames reached me. On the night of January 8th, I saw the worst: families like Maral Nazarian and her husband watched their dream homes turn into nothing but charred ashes. For them, a whole life collapsed in a few hours. It was clear that this wasn't just a fluke. From the coast (Palisades) to the mountains (me, Altadena), the wildfires were no longer just seasonal. They are an everyday threat. The long drought, the strong winds, and climate change have made us all extremely fragile. This is the big lesson I learned from the destruction: We can’t just rely on luck anymore. Science and data are our new shield. Tools like NASA’s satellite images can tell us exactly where the danger is. This data is the key. It's the difference between a city that falls apart in hours, and one that stands strong and ready when disaster strikes. I am Altadena. I'm hurting, but I'm learning. I won't just hope for the best; I will use Science to protect the people who call me home.
